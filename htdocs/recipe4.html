Chocolate Cake with Itallian Meringue

Procedure

  1. Bring 1 cup of milk to a boil, turn off the heat then whisk in the cocoa powder.
  2. Let cool for a bit and finely dice a high quality chocolate bar.
  3. Whisk in the brown sugar and the sour cream into the pan, then add oil, vanilla, and eggs. Mix everything together.
  4. When finely mixed, add flour, baking powder, baking soda and salt.
  5. Mix and combine then whisk in the chocolate chunks. Pour the batter into two 9 or 10 inch cake pans lined with parchment paper and bake at 350F for 40 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  6. Let cool to room temp. before removing from the pan. Move to a baking tray and remove any parchment paper sticking to the cake.
  7. In a large bowl, crack in 4 egg whites. Be careful not to let any of the yolk mix in.
  8. Beat the egg whites until soft peaks appear. Add cream of tartar and corn starch.
  9. Mix the sugar, water, and optionally corn syrup into a saucepan then bring to a boil on high heat.
  10. When the syrup reaches 240-245F, drizzle onto egg whites while beating with a hand mixer.
  11. Keep beating for around 3 minutes or until it no longer takes more volume. Add a tiny splash of vanilla.
  12. Place the first cake on a plate then spread the frosting on top.
  13. Layer the 2nd cake on top along with all of the remaining frosting.
  14. Spread the meringue all over the cake. Top with any sweets you’d like, sprinkles or other such sweets would be good. Test link Test link

This is a delicious chocolate cake that uses a special kind of frosting. Italian Meringue tastes like marshmallows, and probably is made of marshmallows. This recipe, while not the healthiest, is a decadent dish that will please any crowd of people with a soft, dense foundation and a creamy frosting that is a little bit more creative than regular frosting. For beginner cooks or bakers, this is a step up from the other dishes I posted and is a great learning experience. While it looks like any kid’s dream to eat, it is also a dish that is good for adults, too.

Ingredients:

Feeds Two

For the Cake Batter:

  • 1 cup milk
  • 1.5 cups cocoa powder
  • 1 chocolate bar (whatever kind you like)
  • 1.5 cups sugar + a dab of molasses (or the same quantity of brown sugar)
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 8 oz sour cream
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  • 1 cup flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

For the frosting:

  • 4 egg whites
  • 1/2 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1/2 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Squeeze of corn syrup (if you have it)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
