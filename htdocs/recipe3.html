Baked Sushi

Procedure

  1. Prepare and cook the rice. For flavor, add a piece of kombu.
  2. Mix the finished rice with vinegar, salt, sugar and optionally Furikake seasoning.
  3. Shred crabsticks, dice mangoes and julienne cucumbers and carrots.
  4. Toss in a bowl along with Japanese Mayonnaise.
  5. Spread cooked rice onto a casserole and add the mixed kani on top.
  6. Add the egg and cheese on top of this layer.
  7. Bake in a 375F oven for 10-15 minutes or until cheese is bubbly.
  8. Remove from the oven and allow to cool slightly.
  9. Garnish with additional mango, egg, mayonnaise and cucumber.
  10. Enjoy by scooping a portion of the casserole and wrapping it in nori seaweed.

Baked sushi is a great sample of Japanese cuisine but made a lot easier for home cooks while still retaining its signature taste and texture. Instead of rolling rice around various fillings then wrapping, it is instead layers in a baking dish and baked in the oven. It’s not only easy to make but fits different budgets. Low budget options would be simple canned tuna or shredded crabsticks while more fancy options could be salmon, shrimp, crab meat or even various shellfish. This recipe is for those that prefer baking over wrapping their own sushi, or those that do not feel comfortable enough to wrap sushi. This dish can be eaten at any part of a meal or any time of the day, as a snack, appetizer or even a main dish.

Ingredients:

Feeds Two

  • 4 cups cooked short-grain rice
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon suga
  • 1/4 cup Furikake
  • 2 cups Imitation crab (kani), shredded
  • 2 mangoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 1/2 cup Japanese mayonnaise
  • 1/2 cup quick-melt cheese, shredded
  • Nori seaweed sheets
