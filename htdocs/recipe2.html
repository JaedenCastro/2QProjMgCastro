Chicken and Rice Bake

Procedure

  1. Get oven heating to 200C. In a large oven-safe pot, heat an ample coating of olive oil on medium-high heat.
  2. When oil is shimmering, place the chicken into the pot skin side down. Cut an onion into bite-sized pieces while the chicken is browning.
  3. After browning, pull the chicken out to a plate. Fry the onions in the leftover oil. Scrape and deglaze whatever sticks to the bottom of the pot.
  4. While onions are cooking, peel and chop carrots into bite-sized pieces then transfer to the pot.
  5. Cut the chicken into strips, then across into bite sized pieces. (Chicken does not need to be fully cooked at this stage) Add the rice into the pot and let it toast for 1 minute with the carrots and onions.
  6. Before the bottom of the pan burns, add the chicken stock or mix of chicken bouillon and water. Add the chopped chicken into the pot and add herbs and seasonings of your choice. Add pepper and two pinches of salt.
  7. Cover pot with a lid and put it into the preheated oven. Bake for 20-30 minutes, or until rice has absorbed all the moisture. Add frozen peas to warm. Season to taste. Serve with drizzled lemon, lime or kalamansi juice.

This is a nice, healthy, and tasty meal for those that want a big portion of food done in about an hour and may be tired of just the regular Filipino food. Even if I myself love food such as Adobo, I can not say that I would like to eat it all the time and would not want to try other flavors. This recipe is good for those that have an oven and an oven-safe pan. The taste of the dish itself is delicious, providing risotto-like flavors at minimal effort, stress, and time. It is also a very flexible dish, with this recipe being mostly a base where you can add whatever kinds of other seasonings or flavors you want to it. This recipe is perfect for the starting home cook to experiment. Simply put stuff in a pan, bake it a bit, and enjoy.

Ingredients:

Feeds 6-8

  • 1 lb (.5 kg) chicken breast
  • 1 large onion
  • 3-4 carrots
  • 2 cups (350 g) long- or medium-grain white rice
  • 4 cups (32 oz, 830 ml) chicken stock or alternatively 4 cups of water and 2 chicken bouillon cubes
  • 1/2 oz butter
  • 1 cup (150 g) frozen peas
  • 2 teaspoons Herbs de Provence (or other poultry-friendly herbs)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • olive oil, salt, pepper
  • lemons/kalamansi
